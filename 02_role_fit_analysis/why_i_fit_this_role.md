## Differentiators

### 1. 🔬 Research + Engineering Duality
Most interns bring either strong coding skills *or* deep ML knowledge. My M.Eng. research has required both: writing research-quality models *and* engineering clean, reproducible, and scalable code to support them.

### 2. 🤖 Generative AI Initiative
Generative AI is not part of most Electronic Engineering curricula. My self-directed learning — including hands-on experiments with Hugging Face fine-tuning and LLM APIs — demonstrates exactly the learning agility Experian emphasizes.

### 3. 📊 Metric-Driven Evaluation Mindset
My research background means I evaluate models rigorously: ablation studies, statistical significance, multiple metrics. In production ML at Experian, this discipline translates to trustworthy model validation.

### 4. 🤝 Cross-Disciplinary Communication
Electronic Engineering graduate research often sits at the intersection of hardware, software, and data science. I'm practiced at explaining ML results to domain experts who are not data scientists — a valuable skill in a business context.

### 5. ⚡ Fast Ramp-Up Track Record
Graduate research demands constant learning. I've adopted new tools, frameworks, and problem domains rapidly throughout my studies — a direct preview of how I'd perform in a 10-week internship.
