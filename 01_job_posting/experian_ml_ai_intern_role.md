## Role Summary

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Company** | Experian |
| **Team** | Innovation Engineering |
| **Role Title** | ML/AI Software Engineer Intern |
| **Level** | Graduate Internship |
| **Duration** | Summer (typically 10–12 weeks) |
| **Primary Focus** | Developing, training, and evaluating ML/AI models with emphasis on generative AI |

Experian's **Innovation Engineering** team sits at the intersection of applied research and product development. This is not a data-entry or report-generation internship — it is a hands-on **model development and software engineering role** where interns contribute directly to advanced ML pipelines and generative AI experiments.

---

## Full Responsibilities Breakdown

### 1. ML Model Development & Evaluation
> *"Support development, training and evaluation of ML/AI models"*

- You will be writing code to **train models**, tune hyperparameters, and run evaluation loops
- Expect work with **scikit-learn, XGBoost, TensorFlow, and/or PyTorch**
- Model evaluation = metrics (AUC, F1, precision/recall), cross-validation, error analysis

**What this means for you:** Be ready to talk about your own model training workflows and how you measured success.

---

### 2. Supervised Learning Techniques
> *"Logistic regression, gradient boosted machines, random forests"*

- Classical ML remains central — Experian's core products (credit scoring, risk modeling) rely on interpretable, robust supervised models
- GBMs (like XGBoost/LightGBM) are especially important in fintech/credit contexts

**What this means for you:** Highlight any hands-on experience with these specific model families — even if used in academic or research settings.

---

### 3. Generative AI Models
> *"Transformers, diffusion models"*

- This signals the team is actively exploring **LLM applications, synthetic data generation, or document understanding**
- Hugging Face, OpenAI API, or similar GenAI frameworks are likely in use
- Diffusion models may relate to synthetic tabular or image data generation

**What this means for you:** This is a differentiator — candidates with any transformer fine-tuning or GenAI experimentation experience will stand out.

---

### 4. Data Analysis
> *"pandas, NumPy, data visualization"*

- Standard data engineering and exploratory data analysis (EDA) workflows
- Visualization likely involves Matplotlib, Seaborn, or Plotly for presenting findings to stakeholders

**What this means for you:** Frame any research pipeline work as professional data analysis practice.

---

### 5. Software Development
> *"Python, Java, or Kotlin"*

- Python is clearly primary; Java/Kotlin signals potential work with backend services or Android-adjacent products
- Software engineering practices (clean code, version control, reproducibility) are expected

**What this means for you:** Emphasize Python engineering discipline — modular code, Git workflows, reproducible experiments.

---

### 6. Collaboration & Presentation
> *"Present findings and collaborate in innovation projects"*

- Communication skills explicitly valued — you will present to team members and possibly stakeholders
- Innovation projects = ambiguous, exploratory work that requires initiative and structured thinking

**What this means for you:** Prepare stories about presenting technical results to non-technical audiences or cross-functional teams.

## What They Are Really Hiring For

Beyond the bullet points, Experian is looking for three meta-qualities:

### 🔬 1. Intellectual Curiosity + Technical Depth
> Can you independently explore an ML problem, select the right approach, and explain your reasoning?

Experian's Innovation team experiments with cutting-edge methods (GenAI, transformers) while building on proven foundations (XGBoost, logistic regression). They want someone who is **technically grounded but not afraid of novel territory**.

---

### 🤝 2. Collaborative Research Maturity
> Can you work in a team of engineers and researchers, receive feedback, and contribute meaningfully?

The role explicitly mentions "collaborate in innovation projects." Graduate students who have worked in research labs — presenting results, iterating with advisors, and contributing to group deliverables — are ideal.

---

### 🚀 3. Generative AI Awareness
> Do you understand the GenAI landscape and can you apply it responsibly?

Listing both transformers and diffusion models is a signal that the team is actively working in this space. Candidates who can speak fluently about fine-tuning, prompt engineering, embeddings, or synthetic data will be valued significantly above those with only classical ML exposure.

## Strategic Preparation Notes

1. **Lead with Python confidence** — Know your ML stack cold. Be ready to explain a training loop, an evaluation pipeline, or a data preprocessing step without hesitation.

2. **Prepare a GenAI story** — Have at least one concrete example of working with a transformer model, using the Hugging Face library, or experimenting with an LLM-based system.

3. **Frame research as engineering** — Academic projects are real projects. Frame your thesis or research work in terms of business/impact language: what problem did you solve, what was your approach, what was the result?

4. **Emphasize collaboration explicitly** — Experian's Sova behavioral interview explicitly probes this. Have at least two team collaboration stories ready.

5. **Quantify everything you can** — "Improved model accuracy by 8 percentage points," "Reduced preprocessing time by 40%," "Presented to a team of 12 researchers."

6. **Show learning agility** — Be ready to describe a time you rapidly learned a new framework or technique to meet a research or project deadline.
