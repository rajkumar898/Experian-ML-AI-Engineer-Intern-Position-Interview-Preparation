## About the Sova Interview

The **Sova** platform is used by Experian for asynchronous recorded video interviews. You will be presented with behavioral questions one at a time. You typically have:

- **Preparation time:** 30–60 seconds to read the question
- **Recording time:** 90–120 seconds to deliver your answer
- **Retake policy:** Often limited or none — prepare thoroughly in advance

Sova assessments are reviewed by hiring managers and/or AI screening tools. They evaluate:
- Verbal communication clarity
- Structured, logical thinking
- Alignment with company values
- Genuine enthusiasm and professional presence

---

## Sova Delivery Strategy

| Element | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| **Opening** | Start with a brief, confident framing sentence before launching into the story |
| **Structure** | STAR: Situation (10 sec) → Task (10 sec) → Action (40 sec) → Result (20 sec) |
| **Eye contact** | Look directly into the camera lens, not at your own video |
| **Pacing** | Slightly deliberate — give weight to your key points |
| **Energy** | Warm, professional, and engaged — not robotic or overly rehearsed |
| **Closing** | End with a confident connection back to Experian or the role |
| **Environment** | Neutral background, good lighting, minimal background noise |

## Q1 — Why Did You Apply to This Role?

**Experian's Question:**
> *"Each role at Experian plays a key part in helping us achieve our success. Why did you apply to this role?"*

**Experian Value Being Assessed:** Purpose · Impact · Alignment

---

### 🎬 Scripted Answer (≈ 90 seconds)

> *"I applied to this role because it sits at exactly the intersection of the work I've been building toward throughout my graduate studies — machine learning, software engineering, and real-world impact.*
>
> *I'm a Master's student in Electronic Engineering, and my research has focused on developing and evaluating ML models for classification problems. Through that work, I've built a strong foundation in Python, PyTorch, and scikit-learn — and I've developed a genuine passion for generative AI by self-directing experiments with transformer-based models on Hugging Face.*
>
> *What specifically drew me to Experian's Innovation Engineering team is the combination of technical ambition and meaningful application. Experian operates in a domain — credit, identity, and financial data — where the models you build genuinely affect people's lives and economic opportunities. That sense of purpose makes the technical challenges even more compelling.*
>
> *I'm also excited by the team's explicit focus on generative AI and emerging methods. This isn't a role where I'd be applying yesterday's techniques to static problems — it's a place where I'd be exploring what's next and contributing to something genuinely new.*
>
> *I believe my research discipline, my hands-on ML experience, and my eagerness to apply these skills in an innovation-driven environment make me the kind of intern who would add real value — and grow significantly — in this role."*

---

### 📝 Coaching Notes
- Lead with the role intersection (ML + engineering + impact) — not just "I like AI"
- Name-drop specific tools (PyTorch, Hugging Face) naturally — shows it's real
- Connect to Experian's domain with purposeful language (financial impact)
- Close with a confident, forward-looking statement — not a question

## Q2 — Tell Us About a Time You Worked as Part of a Team

**Experian's Question:**
> *"At Experian, we believe in the power of collaboration. Tell us about a time when you worked as part of a team to achieve a goal."*

**Experian Value Being Assessed:** Collaboration · Teamwork · Communication

---

### 🎬 Scripted Answer (≈ 90 seconds)

> *"I'd like to share a collaboration experience from my graduate research project, where I worked as part of a cross-functional team to develop and evaluate a novel machine learning model.*
>
> *Our team of four — two engineers and two domain specialists — was building a classification system for a challenging dataset. The project required integrating domain knowledge from our specialist colleagues with technical model design from the engineering side. Early on, we ran into communication friction: the engineers were speaking in terms of model architecture and loss functions, while the domain specialists needed to understand outputs in terms of their field's existing frameworks.*
>
> *My role was to bridge that gap. I created a shared progress dashboard using simple Python visualizations that translated model performance metrics into domain-interpretable language. I also proposed a weekly 'results review' session where we reviewed model outputs together — not in a technical deep-dive, but in a way that let each team member contribute meaningfully.*
>
> *The result was that both sides of the team stayed aligned throughout the project. We avoided the common research pitfall where engineers optimize for the wrong objective because they don't fully understand the domain. Our final model reflected genuine input from all four team members, and the collaboration style we established became a template for how the lab ran future projects.*
>
> *That experience reinforced for me that strong collaboration isn't just about working side by side — it's about creating conditions where everyone's expertise can actually contribute."*

---

### 📝 Coaching Notes
- Cross-functional team: shows ability to work across disciplines (relevant to Experian's business/tech structure)
- Proactive communication bridge: shows leadership within a team context
- Concrete artifact (dashboard, weekly sessions): specificity builds credibility
- Closing insight: leaves the interviewer with a memorable takeaway

## Q3 — Tell Us About a Time You Had to Learn Something New

**Experian's Question:**
> *"At Experian, we are always learning and improving. Tell us about a time you had to learn something new quickly and apply it effectively."*

**Experian Value Being Assessed:** Learning Agility · Continuous Improvement · Growth Mindset

---

### 🎬 Scripted Answer (≈ 90 seconds)

> *"A great example of fast learning on my part happened when I decided to incorporate transformer-based models into my research — an area that was entirely new to me at the time.*
>
> *My existing research used convolutional architectures, which I knew well. But I kept reading papers showing that transformer-based models — specifically attention mechanisms — were achieving significantly better results on tasks similar to mine. The challenge was that I had no formal training in transformers: no coursework, no prior implementation experience.*
>
> *So I set myself a structured four-week learning plan. In the first week, I worked through the original 'Attention Is All You Need' paper and foundational blog posts to understand the architecture from first principles. In weeks two and three, I implemented a simplified transformer encoder from scratch in PyTorch — not using a pre-built library — so I fully understood each component. In week four, I integrated the Hugging Face `transformers` library into my research pipeline and ran my first fine-tuning experiments.*
>
> *By the end of that month, I had a working transformer-based model outperforming my previous CNN baseline. More importantly, I had deep enough understanding to explain the architecture choices in my research paper and defend them to my supervisor.*
>
> *That experience taught me a learning approach I've carried forward: don't just use a tool until you've understood it at the level where you can explain it and adapt it. That depth is what turns a new skill into a real capability."*

---

### 📝 Coaching Notes
- Specific, verifiable learning sequence — shows genuine effort, not "I watched a YouTube video"
- Implementing from scratch before using the library: signals engineering depth
- Hugging Face mention: directly relevant to Experian's tech stack
- Closing insight: memorable and shows transferable learning methodology

## Q4 — Tell Us About a Time You Found a Creative Way to Solve a Problem

**Experian's Question:**
> *"At Experian, we welcome innovation. Tell us about a time you found a creative or innovative way to solve a problem."*

**Experian Value Being Assessed:** Innovation · Creative Thinking · Resourcefulness

---

### 🎬 Scripted Answer (≈ 90 seconds)

> *"I'd like to share a solution I developed when I was facing a common but difficult research constraint: I had a task that required strong model performance, but my labeled dataset was much too small to train a deep learning model from scratch reliably.*
>
> *The conventional approach would have been to either collect more data — which wasn't feasible — or fall back to classical ML methods that required less data but had a lower performance ceiling. Neither option felt satisfying.*
>
> *Instead, I proposed a hybrid modeling strategy. The key insight was to use self-supervised pre-training on our large pool of unlabeled data to train the model to learn general feature representations — without needing any labels at all. Then, I fine-tuned the pre-trained model on our small labeled dataset. This two-stage approach was borrowed from natural language processing, where pre-training on large corpora before fine-tuning on small downstream datasets had become standard — but it hadn't been applied in my specific research domain before.*
>
> *I prototyped and validated this approach in PyTorch, running controlled ablations to confirm that the pre-training phase was genuinely contributing to downstream performance. The results were clear: the hybrid approach outperformed both the baseline classical model and a deep learning model trained from scratch on just the labeled data.*
>
> *The creative part wasn't inventing something new — it was recognizing that a technique proven in one domain could be transferred and adapted to solve a seemingly unrelated problem in mine. That kind of cross-domain thinking is something I actively try to cultivate."*

---

### 📝 Coaching Notes
- Problem is relatable and real (data scarcity: common in enterprise ML)
- Transfer learning / self-supervised learning: directly maps to Experian's GenAI interests
- Controlled ablations: scientific rigor within a creative approach
- Closing insight: cross-domain thinking is a differentiator and an Experian-aligned value

## Sova Technical Checklist

Use this checklist the day before your Sova interview:

- [ ] Camera is at eye level and in focus
- [ ] Lighting is front-facing (not backlit by a window)
- [ ] Background is neutral and professional (plain wall or tidy bookshelf)
- [ ] Audio is clear — use headphones with a microphone if possible
- [ ] Browser/app is tested and working
- [ ] Notifications and other applications are silenced
- [ ] Answers are practiced out loud — not just memorized on paper
- [ ] Each answer has been timed and fits within 90 seconds
- [ ] You have a glass of water nearby
- [ ] You are dressed professionally (at least from the waist up)
