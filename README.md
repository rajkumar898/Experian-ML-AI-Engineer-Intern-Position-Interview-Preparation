## About This Repository

This repository documents a structured, research-grade approach to preparing for the **Experian ML/AI Software Engineer Internship** interview process. It covers:

- 📄 Full job posting breakdown and "what they're really hiring for" analysis
- 🎯 Role-fit mapping between the candidate's background and the role requirements
- 🧠 Seven mock interview questions with STAR-based model answers
- 🎥 Four actual **Sova video interview** questions with polished 90-second answers
- 📚 A reusable **behavioral story bank** that maps real project experiences to multiple competency dimensions

All answers are calibrated for a **Master's-level candidate** in Electronic Engineering with ML/AI research experience, targeting a **90-second recorded video response format**.

## How to Use This Repo

1. **Start with the job posting analysis** (`01_job_posting/`) to deeply understand what Experian values.
2. **Review the role-fit analysis** (`02_role_fit_analysis/`) to internalize your competitive advantages.
3. **Practice mock questions** (`03_mock_interview_questions/`) out loud — record yourself.
4. **Study and rehearse the Sova answers** (`04_actual_sova_questions/`) targeting 75–90 seconds per response.
5. **Use the story bank** (`05_behavioral_story_bank/`) to adapt your strongest stories to any surprise question.

> 💡 **Tip:** For Sova video interviews, aim for 75–90 seconds, speak at a measured pace, and always end with what you learned or the impact achieved.

## Key Themes to Emphasize

- 🤝 **Collaboration** — Research labs are team environments; demonstrate cross-functional partnership
- 💡 **Innovation** — Highlight novel approaches, hybrid models, or creative pivots in your work
- 📈 **Learning Agility** — Show how quickly you've adopted new frameworks (Hugging Face, transformers)
- 🐍 **Python fluency** — Mention specific libraries naturally and in context
- 🤖 **Generative AI literacy** — Reference transformers, diffusion models, fine-tuning, and prompt engineering
- 📊 **Impact focus** — Always quantify results where possible (accuracy %, time saved, papers/presentations)
